Exploring the North Country

The Adirondack Park

The Adirondack Park is a massive protected area in upstate New York, covering over six million acres. It's the largest publicly protected area in the contiguous United States, larger than Yellowstone, Yosemite, Grand Canyon, Glacier, and Olympic National Parks combined. The park offers endless opportunities for outdoor recreation throughout all four seasons.

Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, fishing, and boating in the summer months. Winter brings opportunities for skiing, snowshoeing, and ice fishing. The park contains over 2,000 miles of hiking trails, 1,200 miles of rivers, and 30,000 miles of streams. Whether you're an experienced outdoors person or just looking for a peaceful retreat, the Adirondacks have something for everyone.

St. Lawrence River

The St. Lawrence River forms part of the border between New York and Canada and is one of the most important waterways in North America. The Thousand Islands region, where the river meets Lake Ontario, is famous for its stunning natural beauty and rich history. The area features over 1,800 islands ranging from large landmasses to tiny outcroppings.

Recreation on the St. Lawrence includes world-class fishing for bass, pike, and muskie. Boating and kayaking are popular activities, with many islands accessible only by water. Historic sites like Boldt Castle draw visitors from around the world. The river corridor offers excellent bird watching opportunities and scenic views that change dramatically with the seasons.

Canton Village

Canton is a charming college town that serves as the home of St. Lawrence University and SUNY Canton. The village's Main Street features historic architecture, local shops, and family-owned restaurants that give it a welcoming small-town feel. The community comes alive during the academic year with cultural events, concerts, and theatrical performances.

Throughout the year, Canton hosts various festivals and events that bring the community together. The Farmers Market runs during the warmer months, featuring local produce and crafts. The village is centrally located, making it an ideal base for exploring other North Country attractions. With its combination of educational institutions, cultural offerings, and natural beauty, Canton represents the best of North Country living.
